Newton's Universal Law of Gravitation

Description:
Isaac Newton had concluded that the magnitude of the force of gravity must decrease with increasing distance between two objects (For example: The Sun and a planet) in proportion to the inverse square of their separation. To determine the gravitational attraction between any two objects, use the equation below.
Equation:
Fg =
G * m1 * m2 / r2
  • F - Gravitational force between two objects
  • G - Gravitational constant
  • m1 - Mass of object one
  • m2 - Mass of object two
  • r - Distance between objects one & two

Calculate a Black Hole's Schwarzschild Radius

Description:
The size of the event horizon of a black hole depends on the mass of the black hole. The greater the mass, the larger the radius of the event horizon. We can use the equation below to determine the Schwarzschild radius of the event horizon.
Equation:
Rs =
2GM / c2
  • Rs - Schwarzschild radius
  • G - Gravitational constant
  • M - Mass of black hole (solar mass(es))
  • M2 - Speed of light
